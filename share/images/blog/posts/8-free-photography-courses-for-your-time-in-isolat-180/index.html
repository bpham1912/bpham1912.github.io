    8 free photography courses for your time in isolation

    Give your creativity a boost by learning a new skill for free!

    Freebies  •  Ngày 06 tháng 4 năm 2020
         

    Across the planet, many people are stuck in isolation. It can be hard to stay creative as a photographer in times like this.

    Fortunately, we've found some great, free courses that have been released by skilled photographers and artists around the world. Whether you're a beginner or an advanced photographer, there's plenty of resources here that will help you take your artistic craft to the next level. 

     

    1. Nikon School- Free for the entire month of April

    Summary: 

    Nikon has released all of its courses free for the next month. While the courses are often focused on Nikon products, a lot of the techniques are easy to reproduce no matter what kind of gear you use. 

    We recommend: 

    The Environmental Portraiture course by Joey Terril was full of fantastic tips - Many of which could be done in your own home.
     

    Link: Click here

     

     2. Leica - #stayathomewithLeica

    Summary:
    The Leica Academy is sharing a series of events with world-class artists which all have a focus on how to document and explore creativity during our time in isolation.

    We recommend:
    Doug Menuez will be doing a talk on how he's documenting his family during his time at home on April 7 (4pm) EDT. 

    Where to find it:  Click here

     

    3. Harvard University-Digital Photography Exposed

    Summary: 

    This 12 part course has been around for a few years however it remains great for beginner to the intermediate photographers. Many advanced photographers will learn a thing or two too. There's some fun projects and assignments as part of the course for you to test out your skills. We would love to see your projects on Pixabay!

    We recommend: 

    While it was very technical, we loved the lecture on histograms which you can find here:

    Click here

    Link: 

    Click here

    4. Lighting Essentials - free Ebook and Videos By The University of NSW

    Summary: 

    This is a wonderful resource that seems to have been hidden away on the internet for many years. While the course is designed for videographers, there's plenty of great tips for still photographers who want to learn more about studio lighting. 

    We recommend: 

    The short video on reflectors is fantastic. Learning how to place and use a reflector is a simple skill and yet is one of the most valuable skills a photographer or videographer can learn for off-camera lighting. It's one minute of your time that would be very well spent. 

    Where to find it:

    Free Book: Click here

    Instructional Videos:  Click here

     

    5. The museum of Modern Art (MoMA) -Photography courses

    Summary: 

    You may already know of MoMA, a bastion of beautiful contemporary art. However, the museum also curates and hosts many online courses on e-learning site coursera.com

    Where to find it: Moma hosts its courses on the course platform site Coursera. Head to Coursera and you can find a whole range of photography courses, MoMA courses and a whole lot more.

    Click here 

    We recommend: 

    'Seeing Through Photographs' by Sarah Meister, Curator at MoMA will change the way you think about your work. Warning: You will never think about your art the same way again after participating in this course.

    Click here

     

    6. Photocritic Intro to Photography

    Summary: 

    Photocritic runs a year-long, 21 lesson photography course with assignments and feedback. Each lesson will be sent to your inbox once a week. It's always been free, so you can probably try this course out in the future. 

    We recommend:
    We really enjoyed the "Learning to look” lesson which gave a great outline of how to make the most out of any shooting situation. 

    Where to find it: You can sign up for the course if you Click here

     

    7. Skillshare- Flat Lay photography course

    (Free membership for people experiencing Hardship from COVID-19)

    Summary:

    Skillshare has a huge array of courses most of which cover a single photographic technique in an easy to understand format. Typically you have to pay a subscription fee; however, the organisation is currently offering a free subscription for people financially affected by COVID-19.

    We recommend: 

    The Donut Flat Lays course gives a great outline of how to shoot "flat lay” photography, one of the most popular techniques on social media. Check it out: Click here

    Where to find it: To fill out the application, click here. It takes around 15 minutes to complete.

    To check out some of the other photography courses you can Click here

     

    8. LinkedIn Learning (formerly Lynda.com) free courses for Library members

    Summary: Many libraries across America, Australia, The United Kingdom and Europe provide free access to courses in LinkedIn Learning for cardholders. To check if you're eligible for free access just head to your local library website, or ask to borrow your friends' library card - don't worry we won't tell anyone!

    We recommend: 
    Sean Duggan's short course on how to capture better photos with your iPhone will help you get results on your mobile device that are almost as good as a professional camera. 
    Check it out here: Click here
